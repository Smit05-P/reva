### What is a Fissure?
- Definition: Small tear in the anal lining from trauma or excessive stretching during bowel movements
- Leads to: Sharp pain and bleeding during/after defecation
- Affects: Individuals of any age

---

### Causes Section
- Constipation and Straining
- Diarrhea
- Childbirth (vaginal delivery strain)
- Trauma / injury to anal area
- Tight Anal Sphincter Muscles (hypertonic)
- Underlying Conditions (e.g., Crohn's disease, IBD)
- Infection in anal region

---

### Symptoms Section
- Sharp, stinging/burning pain during and after bowel movements (lasting hours)
- Bright red bleeding on toilet paper or stool surface
- Visible tear/crack in skin around anus
- Anal sphincter spasms
- Itching or irritation around anus

### Treatment Section

#### Conservative Management
- Dietary changes (high fiber)
- Hydration (soft stools)
- Stool softeners (OTC)
- Sitz baths (warm water, muscle relaxation)
- Topical medications
- Proper hygiene after bowel movements

#### Surgical Options (for Chronic Fissure)

| Procedure | Description | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|---|---|---|
| Laser Fissurectomy | Minimally invasive laser removal | Less pain, faster recovery, low infection risk | Higher cost, limited availability, potential recurrence |
| Lateral Internal Sphincterotomy (LIS) | Cutting small portion of internal sphincter | High success rate, quick relief, low recurrence | Slight incontinence risk |
| Fissurectomy | Surgical removal of damaged tissue | Effective for persistent fissures | Longer healing, scar tissue risk |
| Botox Injections | Temporary sphincter paralysis | Non-invasive, minimal downtime | Temporary results, may need repeat injections |
